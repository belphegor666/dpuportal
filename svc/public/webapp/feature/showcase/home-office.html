Digital Policing - Notting Hill Carrnival

The challenge

How can we innovate policing to make the Notting Hill Carnival a safer event where people can have fun and the police can develop its community links.

The answer

Using Digital Technologies, we can keep people Informed, Aware, Empowered and Protected

Working with the community

The app allows people to monitor what is happening at the Carnival and report incidents.

When an incident is reported:
The location on the map where the incident took place is entered from the device or manually.
Events are categorised, different incident types to be colour coded on the map
Status can be tracked: reported, confirmed, closed, ongoing

Proactive policing

Officers can access Drone Cam to get an areal view of potential build ups and incidents.

Potential troublemakers or other persons of interest may be identified either:
visually by officers on the ground and reported or
via technology (CCTV, drone, wearables) by an operator or
using facial recognition software.
The location of the person when identified will be added to the map with relevant metadata.

A digital tag could be created for the subject and their subsequent movements tracked.

Engagement before, during and after the event

The app can be used to get people involved with supporting a safe and enjoyable carnival.

The vast majority of people want to attend the event to have fun and be entertained. The app will allow people to:
Get involved in the event in advance.
Contribute to a safe event.
Discourage criminal activity.

It is well known that the greatest deterrent of crime is a high likelihood of detection. If the community are able to contribute to the policing of the event, then the high probability of detection should lead to a more enjoyable event for all and lower crime rates.
